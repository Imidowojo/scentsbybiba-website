Fragrance Education

How to Wear Your Scent

Master the art of fragrance application to make every spray count. These tips will help your perfume last longer and project beautifully.

01

Know Your Pulse Points

Apply perfume where your blood vessels are closest to the skin for maximum warmth and projection.

The best pulse points are: the inner wrists, behind the ears, the base of the throat, inside the elbows, and behind the knees. These warm areas help diffuse the fragrance naturally throughout the day. Avoid rubbing your wrists together — this crushes the top notes and changes how the scent develops.
Read more
02

Layer Your Fragrances

Combine complementary scents to create a fragrance profile that is uniquely and distinctively yours.

Start with a scented body wash or lotion as your base layer, then apply your perfume on top. For advanced layering, combine two fragrances: apply a heavier, richer scent first (like oud or vanilla), then finish with a lighter note (like citrus or floral). This creates depth and complexity that a single perfume alone cannot achieve.
Read more
03

Store Correctly

How you store your perfume directly impacts how long it retains its original character and quality.

Keep your perfumes in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight and humidity — your bathroom is actually the worst place. A bedroom drawer or closet shelf is ideal. Always keep the cap on to prevent oxidation. Stored correctly, a quality fragrance can last 3–5 years without losing its potency or character.
Read more
04

Timing is Everything

Apply your fragrance right after a shower for the longest-lasting scent experience possible.

Moisturized skin holds fragrance much longer than dry skin. After showering, apply an unscented moisturizer or body oil, then spray your perfume while your skin is still slightly damp. The moisture helps lock in the scent molecules. In Lagos's warm climate, opt for 2–3 sprays rather than drenching — heat amplifies fragrance naturally.
